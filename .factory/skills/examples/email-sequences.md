# Email Sequences

Complete templates for Soap Opera and Daily Seinfeld sequences.

All sequences must pass the Truth Gate before use. Urgency should only be used when real.

---

## Soap Opera Sequence (5 Emails)

Bond-building sequence for new subscribers. Creates emotional connection through storytelling.

### Email 1: Set the Stage

**Purpose**: Introduce conflict, establish rapport, open loop

**Template**:

```
Subject: [PERSONAL/VULNERABLE OPENER]

Hey [NAME],

[GREETING + ACKNOWLEDGE THEIR ACTION]

I need to tell you something that most [CATEGORY] won't admit...

[CONFESSION/VULNERABLE MOMENT]

I wasn't always [CURRENT SUCCESSFUL STATE].

In fact, [TIME AGO], I was [STRUGGLING STATE].

[SPECIFIC PAINFUL DETAIL]

Tomorrow, I'll tell you what happened next—and why it changed everything
for me.

But for now, I want you to know one thing:

[EMPATHY STATEMENT ABOUT THEIR SITUATION]

Talk soon,
[NAME]

P.S. [HINT AT TOMORROW'S REVELATION]
```

**Example**:

```
Subject: I almost deleted this email

Hey Sarah,

Welcome to the list. Glad you grabbed the guide.

I need to tell you something that most "marketing gurus" won't admit...

I failed for 3 years before anything worked.

I wasn't always running a 7-figure agency.

In fact, 4 years ago, I was $47,000 in debt, living in my sister's
basement, refreshing my inbox hoping someone—anyone—would respond
to my cold emails.

The rejection was constant. The self-doubt was crushing.

Tomorrow, I'll tell you what happened next—and why it changed
everything for me.

But for now, I want you to know one thing:

If you're struggling right now, it doesn't mean you're not cut out
for this. It might mean you're exactly like I was—one insight away
from breakthrough.

Talk soon,
Jake

P.S. The turning point came from the most unexpected place. I'll
share the full story tomorrow.
```

### Email 2: High Drama

**Purpose**: Backstory climax, tension peak, deepen connection

**Template**:

```
Subject: [DRAMATIC MOMENT TEASER]

[NAME],

Yesterday I told you about [REFERENCE TO EMAIL 1].

Here's what happened next...

[DRAMATIC SCENE SETTING]

I remember [SPECIFIC SENSORY DETAIL].

[THE LOWEST POINT / CRISIS MOMENT]

That's when [UNEXPECTED THING HAPPENED].

[DESCRIBE THE MOMENT OF TENSION]

I had a choice: [OPTION A] or [OPTION B].

I chose [OPTION], and it led me to discover something that
[CHANGED EVERYTHING].

But I didn't know that yet.

Tomorrow, I'll share the exact moment everything clicked—and the
unlikely source of the breakthrough.

[SIGN OFF]

P.S. [TEASE THE NEXT EMAIL]
```

**Example**:

```
Subject: The email that made me cry

Jake,

Yesterday I told you about being $47k in debt.

Here's what happened next...

It was 2:47 AM. I know because I was staring at the clock, unable
to sleep again.

I remember the glow of my laptop being the only light in the basement.

That night, I got my 47th rejection in a row. A prospect I'd spent
3 weeks nurturing replied with three words: "Not interested. Unsubscribe."

I actually cried.

That's when I got an email from someone I'd never heard of—a guy
named Marcus who found my blog post from 2 years ago.

He didn't want to hire me. He wanted to tell me I was doing
everything wrong.

I had a choice: Ignore him or listen.

I almost deleted it. Almost.

Tomorrow, I'll share the exact advice Marcus gave me—and why it
was the opposite of what every "expert" teaches.

Talk soon,
Jake

P.S. Marcus's one sentence changed my entire approach. You might
want to write it down.
```

### Email 3: Epiphany

**Purpose**: Discovery moment, solution appears, hope emerges

**Template**:

```
Subject: [THE REVELATION / AHA MOMENT]

[NAME],

Remember [UNEXPECTED PERSON/SOURCE] from yesterday?

Here's what they told me:

"[THE KEY INSIGHT/QUOTE]"

At first, I didn't get it.

[EXPLAIN YOUR INITIAL REACTION]

But then I tried [SPECIFIC ACTION].

And [WHAT HAPPENED].

[DESCRIBE THE EPIPHANY MOMENT]

It wasn't about [COMMON BELIEF].
It was about [ACTUAL TRUTH].

This one shift [RESULT].

Tomorrow, I'll show you the hidden benefits I didn't expect—things
that went far beyond [OBVIOUS BENEFIT].

[SIGN OFF]

P.S. [TEASE UNEXPECTED BENEFITS]
```

**Example**:

```
Subject: The one sentence that changed everything

Sarah,

Remember Marcus from yesterday?

Here's what he told me:

"You're solving the wrong problem. Stop trying to get clients.
Start trying to get ONE client to say 'this was the best money I
ever spent.'"

At first, I was annoyed. "That's your advice? Focus on one client?"

I was looking for a tactic. He gave me a philosophy.

But I was desperate. So I tried it.

I emailed my last client—the one who'd ghosted me after our project
ended—and asked: "What would have made this a 10/10 experience?"

What she told me gutted me. And saved my business.

It wasn't about the deliverables.
It was about how I made her FEEL.

This one shift—from "more clients" to "better experience"—led to
7 referrals in 3 months.

Tomorrow, I'll show you the hidden benefits I didn't expect—things
that went far beyond getting clients.

Talk soon,
Jake

P.S. What happened with client #8 made me realize I'd been thinking
about marketing completely backwards.
```

**Template**:

```
[NAME],

So I applied [THE EPIPHANY FROM EMAIL 3].

I expected [OBVIOUS EXPECTED RESULT].

What I didn't expect:

→ [HIDDEN BENEFIT 1]
→ [HIDDEN BENEFIT 2]
→ [HIDDEN BENEFIT 3]

[EXPLAIN THE MOST SURPRISING ONE]

[SOCIAL PROOF: TESTIMONIAL OR CASE STUDY]

Here's the thing nobody tells you about [TOPIC]:

[COUNTERINTUITIVE TRUTH]

Tomorrow, I'm going to share [WHAT YOU'RE OFFERING] with you—the
exact [FRAMEWORK/SYSTEM/METHOD] that made this possible.

But I want you to understand something first...

[MINDSET SHIFT NEEDED]

[SIGN OFF]

P.S. [HINT AT NEXT EMAIL OR OFFER - only if natural]
```

**Integrity note**: Only hint at urgency if it's real and enforceable.

### Email 5: Close + CTA

**Purpose**: Close the loop, provide clear action, use urgency only if real

**Template**:

```
Subject: [DIRECT/DECISION SUBJECT LINE]

[NAME],

Over the last few days, I've shared:

→ [EMAIL 1 SUMMARY - one line]
→ [EMAIL 2 SUMMARY - one line]
→ [EMAIL 3 SUMMARY - one line]
→ [EMAIL 4 SUMMARY - one line]

Now it's decision time.

[INTRODUCE YOUR OFFER]

Here's exactly what you get:

→ [BENEFIT/DELIVERABLE 1]
→ [BENEFIT/DELIVERABLE 2]
→ [BENEFIT/DELIVERABLE 3]

[GUARANTEE]

[IF REAL URGENCY EXISTS: STATE IT HONESTLY]
[IF NO REAL URGENCY: SKIP THIS ENTIRELY]

[PRIMARY CTA BUTTON/LINK]

The decision is yours.

[SIGN OFF]

P.S. [SUMMARIZE MAIN BENEFIT + REPEAT CTA]
```

**Integrity note**: Only include urgency if you have a real, enforceable reason (cohort closes, price change, capacity limit). If you have no real urgency, simply state the offer clearly and respect the reader's timeline. Manufactured urgency destroys trust.

**The decision is yours** — this phrase returns agency to the reader and honors the crossing.

## Daily Seinfeld (Ongoing)

90% entertainment, 10% content. Consistent pre-framing through daily stories.

### Structure

```
Subject: [CURIOSITY-DRIVEN, OFTEN PERSONAL]

[SHORT STORY FROM DAILY LIFE - 3-5 PARAGRAPHS]

[THE LESSON / CONNECTION TO YOUR TOPIC]

[ONE-SENTENCE MENTION OF OFFER - SOFT CTA]

[SIGN OFF]
```

### Example Topics

| Life Event                | Connection              | Soft Plug                      |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Coffee shop mishap        | Customer service lesson | "This is why I teach X"        |
| Kid's soccer game         | Persistence lesson      | "Same principle in my program" |
| Argument with spouse      | Communication lesson    | "Related to what I cover in Y" |
| Netflix binge             | Story structure         | "I use this framework in Z"    |
| Grocery store observation | Human nature            | "Psychology behind my method"  |

### Example Email

```
Subject: The barista who ruined my morning (and taught me everything)

So there I am at Starbucks.

"Grande americano, please."

She looks at me like I just insulted her grandmother.

"We don't have americanos."

Excuse me? This is a STARBUCKS. You literally invented the americano.

Turns out, they were "out of hot water."

I'm not making this up.

Here's the thing: I wasn't mad at her. I was fascinated.

Because she had two choices in that moment:
1. Apologize and offer an alternative
2. Act like I was the problem

She chose #2. And lost a customer for life.

This happens in marketing ALL the time.

When a prospect says "I'm not interested," most people either
grovel or get defensive.

The pros? They get curious.

"That makes sense. Out of curiosity, what would need to be true
for this to be worth a conversation?"

Small shift. Massive difference.

Anyway, that's what I cover in Module 3 of the program. Just one
of about 40 frameworks like this.

Talk soon,
Jake

P.S. Tomorrow I'll tell you about the Uber driver who accidentally
gave me a $10,000 idea. For real.
```

## Key Principles

1. **Open loops**: Always hint at tomorrow (Pregnant Void — paid back in next email)
2. **Specificity**: Details create believability (Living Materials — verifiable facts)
3. **Vulnerability**: Flaws create connection (Authentic Influence — real, not performed)
4. **Entertainment first**: Value before pitch (Earned Entry — earn the right to offer)
5. **Soft CTAs**: Mention offer, don't push (The Threshold — honor the crossing)
6. **Agency preservation**: "The decision is yours" (Sacred Friction — respect choice)
7. **Honest urgency only**: Real deadlines or none (Truth Gate — no manufactured pressure)
